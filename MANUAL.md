4. **Start with all "Critical" tasks first** — they are blockers to later phases.

- **Not reading Phase 4 tasks early** — License applications often require documents that take weeks to prepare. Start reading Phase 4 requirements in Week 1.

- Click **Submit** to add the license to your tracker

- Lists all licenses you've applied for
- Expand each entry to see:
  - Current status (Pending / Approved / Rejected / Renewal Due)
  - Full document checklist
  - **Mark as Done** button — Updates status to Approved
  - Renewal date reminders

---

### Step-by-Step: How to Apply for a License

1. Navigate to **Licensing** from the sidebar.
2. **Scan the license catalog** — Start with "Mandatory" category.
3. **Click a license card** to read the requirements and document checklist.
4. Click **Apply** on the licenses you need.
5. **Select your state** in the modal and click Submit.
6. The license appears in your **License Tracker** (right panel).
7. **Prepare and submit documents** to the relevant authority outside the platform.
8. **Return to the tracker** and click **Mark as Done** once approved.

---

### Tips & Best Practices

- **Start with these 4 mandatory licenses immediately:** CEA Approval, BMW Authorization, Trade License, GST Registration. These have the longest approval timelines.
- **Prepare your documents in parallel** — Don't wait for one license to be approved before applying for the next.
- **Keep scanned copies of all documents** in a dedicated folder (Google Drive or physical file) — you'll need them multiple times.
- **Use Renewal Alerts as a calendar reminder** — Set a phone alarm 30 days before renewal dates.
- **State matters significantly** — licensing authorities, fees, and document requirements vary by state.

---

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Applying for a license before preparing the required documents** — Check the document checklist first. Incomplete applications are rejected and waste weeks.
- **Ignoring optional licenses** — Some "optional" licenses are effectively mandatory for certain test types (e.g., PC-PNDT registration for genetic labs).
- **Not selecting your state** in the Apply modal — The system tailors document requirements to your state. Skipping this gives you a generic list.
- **Forgetting renewal dates** — License expiry means operating illegally. Monitor the Renewal Alerts card daily.
- **Marking licenses as Done before receiving official confirmation** — Only mark as Done when you have the official approval document in hand.

---

### Key Elements on the Page

| Stat | Description |
|---|---|
| **CAPEX Tracking** | Total capital expenditure estimate vs. committed |
| **Procurement %** | Percentage of equipment delivered or beyond |

| Step | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `Planning` | Identified as needed, not yet ordered |
| `Ordered` | Purchase order submitted to vendor |
| `Delivered` | Equipment received at lab |
| `Installed` | Physically set up and operational |

**4 Static Equipment Categories**

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|

| Phlebotomy & Collection | Vacutainer systems, centrifuges, cold chain equipment |

| IT & Infrastructure | LIS terminal, printers, barcode scanners |

**Vendor Marketplace**
- Pre-negotiated vendor discounts (e.g., Mobilab 50%, Sysmex 15%)
- Contact details for each vendor
- Pricing context for budgeting

---

### Step-by-Step: How to Plan Your Equipment

1. Navigate to **Equipment** from the sidebar.
2. **Use the AI Recommendation panel** — Enter your test menu and generate recommendations.
3. **Review the 4 static categories** to ensure nothing is missed.
4. **Add equipment to your list** using the Add button on each item.
5. **Set each item to "Planning" status** initially.
6. **Contact vendors** via the Marketplace for pricing and lead times.
7. **Update status to "Ordered"** once your PO is submitted.
8. **Progress through each stage** as delivery and installation occur.
9. **Monitor CAPEX tracking** to stay within budget.

---

### Tips & Best Practices

- **Run the AI recommendations before manually adding equipment** — you may discover instruments you hadn't considered.
- **Check lead times early** — some imported analyzers (Sysmex, Abbott) have 6–10 week delivery times. Order early.
- **Don't mark equipment "Integrated" until it's connected to your LIS** — this is a compliance requirement, not just a status update.
- **Use the Power Load stat** when planning your lab's electrical infrastructure — share it with your electrician.
- **Track pending calibrations diligently** — un-calibrated equipment is a NABL non-conformance.

---

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Ordering equipment before confirming lab space dimensions** — Some analyzers are large. Measure your bench space first.
- **Ignoring IT infrastructure** — The LIS terminal and barcode scanner are as important as the hematology analyzer. Don't deprioritise them.

- **Leaving items stuck at "Delivered"** — Delivered equipment that isn't installed creates false comfort. Chase installation actively.
- **Not updating CAPEX tracking** — Budget overruns are the #1 cause of lab setup delays. Update costs as quotes come in.
